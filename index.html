Workshop Focus

Why This Workshop Matters Now

Future maritime wireless systems are evolving toward space-air aided architectures where satellites, high-altitude platforms, and UAV systems enhance maritime connectivity, sensing, resilience, and coordinated operations. This workshop is designed around that maritime-centric view rather than a generic all-domain integration narrative.

Workshop Overview

The workshop focuses on theoretical foundations, algorithmic innovations, and system-level design for Space-Air Aided Maritime (SAAM) systems. We welcome both model-driven and data-driven methods spanning integrated sensing and communication, intelligent networking, adaptive resource allocation, distributed AI, and cross-layer optimization under realistic propagation and hardware constraints.

The scope emphasizes sparse maritime infrastructure, sea-surface propagation effects, high-mobility aerial relaying, satellite Doppler dynamics, cross-domain resource coupling, and mission-critical reliability requirements. The goal is to create a focused venue for researchers shaping the next generation of maritime wireless infrastructures.

Differentiating Angle

Many workshops treat space, air, ground, and sea as symmetric components of one integrated architecture. This workshop instead centers on maritime-driven system design, examining how space and aerial layers can intelligently assist maritime communications through cross-domain coordination and practical deployment-aware optimization.

Call for Papers

Topics of Interest

We invite original contributions on communication and networking technologies for maritime-centric space-air-sea architectures. Topics include, but are not limited to, the following themes.

  • High-resolution and low-latency ISAC for cross-domain satellite-UAV-maritime systems
  • Intelligent networking and self-organizing architectures in multi-layer space-air-sea networks
  • Cross-domain resource allocation and spectrum sharing across heterogeneous layers
  • UAV-assisted networking and cooperative communication
  • Satellite-terrestrial-maritime network integration and interoperability
  • Distributed AI and learning-enabled wireless optimization
  • Mobility-aware waveform design and beam management under LEO Doppler and aerial dynamics
  • Edge intelligence and federated learning in multi-layer networks
  • Resilient communication under high Doppler and dynamic topology
  • Nonstationary spatiotemporal modeling and stochastic geometry for cross-domain networks
  • Secure and privacy-preserving communication in cross-domain infrastructures
  • Multi-agent coordination and intelligent decision-making in cross-domain networks
