Call for Papers

Future maritime wireless infrastructures are evolving toward ocean-centric space-air-sea architectures, where low-earth-orbit (LEO) satellites, high-altitude platforms, and UAV systems act as enabling layers to support and enhance maritime communication networks. Rather than treating all domains as symmetric components of a fully integrated system, this paradigm places the maritime network at the core, with space and aerial segments providing extended coverage, sensing capability, relay support and resilience. Such architectures aim to ensure reliable maritime connectivity, environmental awareness and coordinated multi-layer assistance under sparse infrastructure and dynamic sea conditions.

However, integrating space and aerial assistance into maritime systems introduces fundamental cross-domain challenges, including sea-surface propagation effects, satellite Doppler dynamics, high-mobility aerial relaying, large-scale resource coupling, decentralized coordination, and stringent reliability requirements for mission-critical maritime services. Traditional single-domain communication frameworks are inadequate to capture these tightly coupled interactions. There is a pressing need for new communication paradigms that jointly address sensing-enabled maritime awareness, intelligent cross-layer networking, adaptive resource management, and distributed optimization across the space-air-sea continuum, with a clear emphasis on maritime performance as the primary system objective.

This workshop focuses on theoretical foundations, algorithmic innovations, and system-level design of intelligent communication and networking techniques for Space--Air Aided Maritime (SAAM) systems. The workshop welcomes both model-driven approaches and data-driven methodologies, including but not limited to integrated sensing and communication (ISAC), intelligent networking, adaptive resource allocation, distributed AI, and cross-layer optimization under realistic propagation and hardware constraints.

Topics of Interest

  • High-resolution and low-latency ISAC for cross-domain satellite-UAV-maritime systems
  • Intelligent networking and self-organizing architectures in multi-layer space-air-sea networks
  • Cross-domain resource allocation and spectrum sharing across heterogeneous layers
  • UAV-assisted networking and cooperative communication
  • Satellite-terrestrial-maritime network integration and interoperability
  • Distributed AI and learning-enabled wireless optimization
  • Mobility-aware waveform design and beam management under LEO Doppler and aerial dynamics
  • Edge intelligence and federated learning in multi-layer networks
  • Resilient communication under high Doppler and dynamic topology
  • Nonstationary spatiotemporal modeling and stochastic geometry for cross-domain networks
  • Secure and privacy-preserving communication in cross-domain infrastructures
  • Multi-agent coordination and intelligent decision-making in cross-domain networks
