Call for Papers

Future maritime wireless systems are evolving toward space-air aided architectures where satellites, high-altitude platforms, and UAV systems enhance maritime connectivity, sensing, resilience, and coordinated operations. This workshop is designed around that maritime-centric view rather than a generic all-domain integration narrative.

The workshop "Intelligent Communication and Networking for Space-Air Aided Maritime Systems" focuses on maritime-driven space-air-sea architectures, where the maritime network acts as the operational core and satellites, high-altitude platforms, and UAV systems provide enabling support for coverage extension, sensing, resilience, relay assistance, and coordinated operations. Rather than treating all domains as equal components of a generic integrated network, this workshop highlights how space and aerial segments can intelligently assist maritime systems under sparse infrastructure, dynamic sea conditions, and high-mobility scenarios.

Such ocean-centric architectures are expected to support offshore monitoring, maritime transportation, ocean observation, emergency response, and autonomous operations, but they also introduce tightly coupled challenges including sea-surface propagation effects, satellite Doppler dynamics, aerial mobility, cross-domain resource coupling, decentralized coordination, and stringent reliability requirements.

We welcome original contributions on theoretical foundations, algorithmic innovation, intelligent networking, adaptive resource allocation, distributed AI, integrated sensing and communication, and system-level optimization for Space-Air Aided Maritime (SAAM) systems under realistic propagation, mobility, deployment, and hardware constraints.

Topics of Interest

  • High-resolution and low-latency ISAC for cross-domain satellite-UAV-maritime systems
  • Intelligent networking and self-organizing architectures in multi-layer space-air-sea networks
  • Cross-domain resource allocation and spectrum sharing across heterogeneous layers
  • UAV-assisted maritime networking and cooperative communication
  • Satellite-terrestrial-maritime integration and interoperability
  • Distributed AI and learning-enabled wireless optimization
  • Mobility-aware waveform design and beam management under LEO Doppler and aerial dynamics
  • Resilient communication under high Doppler and dynamic topology
  • Secure and privacy-preserving communication in cross-domain infrastructures
  • Multi-agent coordination and intelligent decision-making in cross-domain networks
  • Cross-domain space-air-surface-underwater network architecture and protocol design
  • Integrated above-water and underwater communications for ocean observation systems
  • Underwater acoustic, optical, and hybrid networking with aerial or satellite assistance
  • AUV, UUV, and USV assisted data collection, relaying, and cooperative maritime networking
  • Air-sea-underwater integrated sensing, localization, tracking, and target monitoring
  • Sea-state-aware channel modeling for satellite, aerial, surface, and underwater links
  • Maritime emergency communications, search-and-rescue networking, and resilient offshore systems
